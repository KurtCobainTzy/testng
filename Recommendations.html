Before You Buy
Beginner Buying Tips
Tip 01
Start with Nylon Strings

Classical guitars with nylon strings are gentler on your fingers while you're still building calluses. Less pain = more practice time.

Tip 02
Don't Over-Spend at First

A ₱3,000–5,000 guitar is more than enough to start. Upgrade once you know you're committed. Gear doesn't make you play better — practice does.

Tip 03
Try Before You Buy

If possible, go to a music store and hold the guitar. Make sure the neck feels comfortable in your hand and that the body size suits you.

Tip 04
Check the Action

"Action" means how high the strings are from the fretboard. High action = harder to press = more pain. Ask the store to set it up properly before you leave.

Tip 05
Acoustic First, Electric Later

Learning on acoustic builds more finger strength and technique. Once you've got the basics down, switching to electric is easy and enjoyable.

Tip 06
Don't Forget the Accessories

A tuner, pick, extra strings, and a capo are small investments that make a huge difference. Check the accessories section below for our picks.
